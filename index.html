Alkalinity & Mechanical Constraints

The native soil is a coarse-silty, mixed, superactive, calcareous, thermic Typic Torrifluvent. The native pH ranges from 8.0 to 8.2 (moderately to strongly alkaline). This high alkalinity limits nutrient bioavailability, specifically locking out essential micronutrients like iron.

⚠ Foundation Root Restrictions

Mechanically, the soil exhibits moderately slow permeability. Because of the 1993 concrete foundation and surrounding driveways, only plants with non-invasive, tap-root, or highly structured root systems are permitted. Aggressive shallow-rooted species (willows, poplars, maples) are strictly prohibited.

The Gaia-Inspired Philosophy (Safe & Structured)

Maximizing biological density while enforcing strict safety constraints for family use.

The Three Absolute Constraints

To ensure this self-regulating biological organism functions as a perfectly safe backyard environment, all plant selections pass these three criteria:

1. Zero Toxicity

Every plant is non-toxic to dogs and children. Common toxic desert plants (Oleander, Sago Palms, Texas Mountain Laurel) are strictly excluded.

2. Structural Safety

All trees/shrubs possess non-invasive, tap-root, or well-behaved root systems to protect the concrete slab foundation, patios, and sidewalks.

3. Physical Safety

Spiky, thorny, or jagged plants (true Cacti, sharp Agaves) are eliminated in favor of soft foliage to prevent physical injury during play.

Shifting Microclimates & Shade Evolution

The Western Front Yard (Full Sun)

Facing due West, it receives unmitigated, high-intensity solar radiation. Reflected heat from asphalt and stucco spikes temperatures 10-15° higher than ambient. Demands a rugged, sun-loving approach with staggered evergreen hedges.

The Eastern Backyard (Deep Shade Oasis)

Inherently hospitable with morning sun and afternoon house shade. Crucially, as the Chinese Pistache anchor tree reaches a 30-foot spread, this zone transitions into a *significant dry-shade* environment requiring highly specialized understory plants.

Hydrology & Kid-Friendly Ground Treatments

Engineered applications for a region receiving only 8-10 inches of annual rainfall.

Safe Ground Treatment Strategy

High-Traffic Play Zones (Inorganic)

Instead of sharp crushed gravel that cuts paws and knees, use smooth, rounded river rock or "pea gravel" for pathways and dog-relief areas. It drains instantly and stabilizes soil against wind.

Planting Beds (Deep Organic Mulch)

3-to-4-inch layer of double-shredded hardwood bark or cedar. Strictly avoid cocoa bean mulch (highly toxic to dogs). Decomposing wood chips build topsoil, buffer pH, and insulate moisture.

Hydrozoning Schematics

Valve Zone Name Watering Strategy
Valve 1 The Shade Oasis (Backyard) Pistache, Hawthorn, Redbud. Moderate frequency, deep saturation.
Valve 2 The Perimeter (Borders) Little Ollie Hedges. Deep, infrequent to establish structural roots. Let top soil dry.
Valve 3 Sun Structural (Front Yard) Texas Ranger, Sage, Dalea. Extremely infrequent. Soil must dry completely between cycles.
